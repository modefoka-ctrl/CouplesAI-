Tips for Better Prompts
1 — Tension first
Pick tension before anything else. It unlocks the affinity system — curated locations, poses and looks matched to that tension appear at the top of every dropdown. Skipping tension produces flat, unconnected prompts. World and tension can both be locked independently.
2 — Stack contrasts deliberately
The best prompts have internal contradiction. A formal wardrobe against a raw pose, or an intimate tension inside a vast location, creates visual friction the model has to resolve — and that resolution is where interesting images come from. Try affinity suggestions paired with off-affinity choices intentionally.
3 — Lock & Spin Unlocked
Lock any field with the padlock — including World and Tension. Then hit Spin Unlocked to explore every pose, look and lighting combination within your locked anchor. You get the full affinity space without losing what's working.
4 — Mood worlds auto-set lighting
Selecting DUSK, SPRING, ROMANCE, GOLD, NIGHT, RAIN or COLD automatically sets lighting to ✦ AI decides. These worlds already embed lighting in their tension phrases and locations — adding an explicit lighting choice on top creates redundancy. Override manually if you want explicit control.
5 — ✦ AI decides buttons
Every field has a ✦ AI button. Tap it to instantly set that field to AI decides — no dropdown needed. Use it on pose when your manual choice feels stiff, or on wardrobe when you want the model to interpret the world freely. Setting 2–3 fields manually and leaving 1–2 to AI often produces the strongest results.
6 — Shot type front-loads composition
Shot type appears first in the prompt — image models weight early tokens most heavily. Extreme close-up forces focus on faces. Wide environmental makes the world as important as the subjects. Low angle heroic changes power and posture. Set shot type first if composition matters most.
7 — Prompts are prose, not tags
The generator builds one connected sentence: tension phrase — pose [connector] location, wearing look, bathed in lighting. The connector (amid, drawn toward, lost in, burning through) is world-specific and fuses pose and location into a single image rather than two separate facts. Rewrite & Tighten restructures this sentence five different ways.
8 — Specificity beats completeness
A sharp tension + one strong location often beats all seven fields filled with mediocre choices. The fixed prefix already establishes format and subject. Fill the fields you care about and let AI interpret the rest. The quality hint bar tells you when you have enough.
9 — Rich tension–world pairings
Some combinations have the deepest affinity maps: GLASS × real vs. reflected · STAGE × spotlight vs. shadow · MYSTERY × face vs. mask · SPACE × gravity vs. weightless · ROMANCE × spoken vs. silent · DUSK × brief vs. eternal · WEDDING × vows vs. doubt · PROTEST × individual vs. crowd · RUNWAY × clothes vs. body · DESERT × vast vs. alone · JUNGLE × ancient vs. alive · KITCHEN × ritual vs. survival.
10 — Same tension, different worlds
Many tensions appear across multiple worlds with different visual vocabularies. Generate in DUST then MOUNTAIN — same emotional core, completely different images. DUSK tensions graft especially well onto TRAVEL or WILD locations for cross-world blending.
11 — Era as tonal modifier
Era appends a period and palette modifier at the end of the prompt. 1970s on Mountain nudges earth tones and film grain. 1980s on City pushes neon and hard shadow. Use it when a prompt feels generic or when you want to anchor the model to a specific visual decade.
12 — Tension search filter
With 36 worlds and 720+ tensions, the search box above the tension dropdown lets you filter instantly by keyword. Type grief, heat, silence, crowd or any emotional word to find matching tensions across the current world's 20 options. Clears automatically when you switch worlds.
13 — Coherence badge & tooltip
The coherence badge (Cohesive / Moderate / Scattered) in the prompt bar scores your combination using the affinity system — not just whether items belong to the world, but whether your specific location, pose and look appear in your tension's curated affinity map. Hover or tap the badge to see exactly why it scored the way it did.
14 — Prompt history persists
Your last 20 prompts are saved automatically to your browser and survive page refreshes and closed tabs. Tap any history item to copy it instantly. Use Clear all to reset. History is stored locally — it never leaves your device.
15 — Manual cross-world blending
Generate a prompt in GOLD, copy it. Generate in NIGHT. Manually combine the tension from one with the location from the other. The generator doesn't cross worlds automatically — but you can by hand in the copied text.
Rewrite & Tighten — 5 Variants
Each variant restructures the same selections using a different editorial strategy. The order elements appear changes how the image model weights them. All 5 are shown in order — copy the one that matches your intent.
Variant 1 — Cinematic Scale
Tension leads, pose fused with location by a connector. Best for epic, wide or emotionally charged compositions. The translated tension phrase sets the emotional register first.

"calm at the center of the explosion — slow motion walk from fire burning through collapsing building exterior, wearing tactical vest sleeves torn."
Variant 2 — Intimacy
Pose leads, location frames, tension arrives last as subtext. Body language before setting. Best for close, personal shots where emotional exchange between subjects matters most.

"tight two-shot. foreheads touching eyes closed, inside candlelit table for two — nerves and certainty held in the same breath, dressed in simple sundress linen shirt."
Variant 3 — Essential
Tension as standalone statement, pose and location fused into one image. Most compressed variant. Best when two elements are strong and you want nothing competing.

"human made small by the vast. floating face to face weightless adrift in space station observation cupola, cold blue moonlight."
Variant 4 — Location-Led
World built first, action placed inside it. The model builds the environment before placing the figures. Best for atmospheric, travel or landscape-dominant images.

"Marrakech medina alley, where both looking at same map confused, tourist stepping into local life, wearing travel linen light layers."
Variant 5 — Wardrobe-Led
Opens with "Dressed in..." — look defines the register first. Best when wardrobe is strong and distinctive: historical costume, carnival dress, tactical gear, editorial fashion. Tension arrives last as emotional colour.

"Dressed in white dress soft veil, vows exchange close eye contact framed by chapel altar candlelit. the weight of a vow spoken over quiet doubt."
Which variant to use
Epic or emotionally charged → Variant 1 (Cinematic Scale)
Close moment, body language first → Variant 2 (Intimacy)
Two perfect elements, strip the rest → Variant 3 (Essential)
Setting is the star → Variant 4 (Location-Led)
Wardrobe defines the image → Variant 5 (Wardrobe-Led)

Copy all 5 into your image model and compare — the same selections produce dramatically different compositions depending on what the model reads first.
